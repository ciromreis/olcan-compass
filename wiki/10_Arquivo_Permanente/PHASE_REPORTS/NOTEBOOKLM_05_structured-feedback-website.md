#### **Global CSS Rule for Timeline Images (No Cropping):**

When injecting these images into the modal or expanded timeline view, the IDE must use:

\<div className="relative w-full h-\[50vh\] md:h-\[70vh\] max-h-\[800px\] bg-slate-950/80 rounded-xl flex items-center justify-center p-4"\>  
  \<Image  
    src={item.image\_path}  
    alt={item.alt\_text}  
    fill  
    className="object-contain drop-shadow-2xl" // KEY: object-contain prevents cropping  
    sizes="(max-width: 768px) 100vw, 80vw"  
    priority  
  /\>  
\</div\>

*Why this matters:* `object-contain` ensures the entire photograph fits within the bounds of the screen, maintaining its original aspect ratio. The dark `bg-slate-950/80` creates a high-end, gallery-like "matting" around the image if the screen ratio differs from the photo.
